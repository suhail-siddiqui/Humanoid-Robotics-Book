# Quiz: Lesson 2.1 - Physical Design of Humanoid Robots

## Question 1
What is the primary challenge in maintaining balance for humanoid robots?

A) Too many degrees of freedom
B) The inherently unstable configuration due to bipedal stance
C) Excessive weight distribution
D) Complex actuator systems

**Answer: B**

## Question 2
Which design consideration involves balancing human-like appearance with functional efficiency?

A) Degrees of Freedom allocation
B) Power and Energy Management
C) Anthropomorphism vs. Functional Efficiency
D) Structural Material Selection

**Answer: C**

## Question 3
What does DOF stand for in the context of humanoid robotics?

A) Dynamic Operational Force
B) Degrees of Freedom
C) Design Optimization Framework
D) Digital Operational Function

**Answer: B**

## Question 4
Which of the following is NOT a key design trade-off in humanoid robotics?

A) Weight vs. Durability
B) Complexity vs. Reliability
C) Speed vs. Precision
D) Temperature vs. Humidity

**Answer: D**

## Question 5
What is the primary purpose of modularity in humanoid robot design?

A) To make robots look more human-like
B) To increase operational speed
C) To enable easier maintenance, upgrades, and customization
D) To reduce material costs

**Answer: C**

## Question 6
Which robot was highlighted as focusing on dynamic mobility and manipulation in challenging environments?

A) ASIMO
B) NAO
C) Atlas
D) Pepper

**Answer: C**

## Question 7
What is a primary concern with increasing the number of degrees of freedom in a humanoid robot?

A) Reduced structural strength
B) Increased system complexity and potential failure points
C) Decreased mobility
D) Lower power consumption

**Answer: B**

## Question 8
Which design methodology focuses on incorporating biological inspiration for efficient movement?

A) Iterative Design Process
B) Modularity Approach
C) Biomimetic Design
D) System Integration Focus

**Answer: C**

## Question 9
What is the main benefit of having a lower center of mass in humanoid robot design?

A) Increased speed
B) Enhanced dexterity
C) Improved stability
D) Reduced power consumption

**Answer: C**

## Question 10
Which of the following best describes the purpose of the iterative design process in humanoid robotics?

A) To reduce material costs
B) To ensure compliance with safety standards
C) To validate concepts early and refine approaches based on empirical results
D) To make robots more anthropomorphic

**Answer: C**
